Akihabara
秋葉原 — Electric Town
Full day. Start at Radio Kaikan, work the main strip, then side streets. Best finds are often off the main drag.
🔒 LOCKERS — Akihabara Station has coin lockers at both exits. 7-Eleven stores in the area now have locker space — look for the locker kiosk inside. Drop bags before shopping to travel light. ¥400–¥700 for station lockers depending on size.

Tips & Cheat Sheet
ヒント · 旅のコツ
Transport
Trains & IC CardTrains stop running around midnight — plan nights accordingly. Rush hour: weekdays 7–9 AM and 5–7 PM — packed, avoid if possible. Get Suica or Pasmo IC card at the airport immediately, or download the Suica app before leaving. Loads on all trains, buses, and most convenience stores.
Luggage Delivery — Yamato Transport (クロネコヤマト)Available at any convenience store. Next-day delivery if sent before 4 PM. Use to ship boxes home from the hotel rather than checking additional bags.
Cash & Payments
CashMost small shops and secondhand stores are cash only. Exchange approximately $500–$700 before or on arrival. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs are most reliable for international cards. Budget ¥100,000+ for a full collector trip. Get a coin pouch — coins accumulate fast.
At RestaurantsBring the check to the front counter to pay — don't wait at the table. Put cash or card in the small tray provided — not directly in someone's hand.
Tax-FreePassport required at electronics and some hobby stores. Over ¥5,000 per purchase qualifies — always ask at the register. !! Tax-free bags get sealed and cannot be opened until you leave the country !!
Gachapon CoinsCollect ¥100 coins from Day 1 — break bills at convenience stores. 50–100 coins minimum before Sunshine City Ikebukuro gachapon hall.
Etiquette
Etiquette BasicsDon't walk and eat · Don't tip — ever · Don't talk loudly in public spaces · Don't point with your fingers · Pay at the front counter, not at the table · Put payment in the small tray — not directly in someone's hand
Practical MiscEat expensive meals at lunch — many places have affordable lunch specials. Bring hand tissues and mini soap — public bathrooms often have no paper towels. No trash cans in the streets — carry a small bag for trash.
In-Store Rules
Bag Check at MandarakeEvery Mandarake location requires bag in a locker at the entrance. Bring a small tote to leave behind. Valuables stay in pockets.
Adult ContentCompletely normalized — sold openly in dedicated store sections. Professional staff, standard retail experience. 18+, some locations ask for ID or passport. Best: Mandarake Akihabara adult floors, M's.
Tattoos & OnsensMany onsens and gyms ban visible tattoos. Look specifically for タトゥーOK locations or book a private bath. Thermae-Yu (Kabukicho, walking distance from Hotel Groove Shinjuku) — verify tattoo policy before going.
Logistics & Shipping
Shipping HomeJapan Post EMS — reliable, international, expensive. Yamato Transport (クロネコヤマト) from any convenience store for domestic boxes. Bring a foldable soft duffel in luggage for overflow. Ship a box from the hotel if needed.
April WeatherHighs ~17–21°C (63–70°F), mild and pleasant. Cherry blossom season ending — late bloomers possible. Light rain possible — compact umbrella or ¥500–¥800 from convenience store.
Flea Market Rain CheckTokyo City Flea Market is cancelled in rain. Check weather the night before April 25. If cancelled, move to April 26 and rearrange the schedule accordingly.
Sizing
Clothing Reality CheckStandard Japanese L ≈ US S/M. Sakazen (Shinjuku + Ikebukuro) is the best bet for larger Western builds — XS to 4XL. Uniqlo Ginza flagship carries up to XXL. International flagships at Omotesando/Ginza carry Western sizing. Anime merch tees from Animate/Jump Shop run 1–2 sizes small.
Shoe Sizing Reality CheckJapanese standard tops out at 27–28cm (≈ US 9–10) in most regular retail. US 12+ is difficult. US 13+ requires specialty stores: Kutsu no Hikari or Big-b — look up both locations before leaving home. Nike Harajuku and Atmos are the best mainstream bets for larger sizes.
